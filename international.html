English as a Second Language Resources

All classes at Washington University will be taught in English with the exception of upper level language classes. Before applying for admission you will be required to take the TOEFL, a test that will ensure you have the English proficiency to succeed. To further strengthen your skills, we recommend checking out the following ESL resources :

First Steps Towards Integrating

To be a successful student and young professional, integrate into the WU, St. Louis, and U.S. cultures:

  • Visit local attractions: the Arch, MO Botanical Garden, Grants Farm, Katy Trail, St. Louis Cardinals, etc.
  • Follow local and national news through stltoday.com, stlamerican.com, NPR (90.7 FM)
  • Enjoy neighborhood food and culture on the Hill, South Grand, Central West End, etc.
  • Attend local festivals such as the St. Louis Art Fair, The Forest Park Balloon Glow, and LouFest.
  • Carve a pumpkin for Halloween and celebrate Thanksgiving with an American family.
  • Volunteer in the St. Louis community through the Campus Y or Community Service Office.
